# Review of `writing/almost_ready.pdf` / 双语审稿意见

## 中文审稿意见

### A. 高优先级逻辑冲突与结论漏洞

1. 研究数量前后不一致。摘要写的是 `two crowdsourced studies`，引言写 `three studies`，Section 3 又写 `two online, crowdsourced studies`，Section 6 写 `across all three studies`。读者无法判断第三个 study 到底是什么，也无法判断 factor analysis 的数据来源。
2. 关于“interaction quality / affordance 不重要”的结论与结果冲突。摘要、Section 3、Section 7、Section 8 都在暗示“只要有 interaction 就会提高 trust，甚至 buggy interaction 也一样”，但 Study 1 明确报告 `Full Interaction > Buggy / No Interaction`，且 `Buggy` 不显著高于 `No Interaction`；Study 2 也明确显示 `click / hover > animation`。这类结论需要全部降调为“某些交互设计与更高 trust 相关”，不能写成“regardless of interaction quality/affordance”。
3. 关于“decision making / calibrated decisions / trustworthy interpretation”的主张明显过强。两项 study 的 prediction tasks 大多是空结果或接近显著，文中主要支持的是主观 trust / confidence / interaction experience 的变化，而不是决策质量提升。
4. 关于 `show-one` 提高 `confidence` 的结论没有被正文结果充分支持。Study 2 讨论、摘要、设计启示都说“revealing less information at a time”会提高 trust 和 confidence，但 Study 2 正文没有给出对应的 confidence 统计检验。
5. 多处把“相关”写成“机制”。例如 “through reducing perceptual load”, “user agency calibrates trust”, “interaction improves trust by giving users control” 都是机制性表述，但文中没有直接测量 perceptual load，也没有做 mediation / mechanism analysis。
6. 方法上存在明显顺序混淆。Study 1 和 Study 2 的 baseline trial 都固定在第一个，baseline 与后续 trials 同时混杂了练习效应、熟悉度、疲劳和条件差异；后文只加了 baseline indicator，但这不能完全消除 order confound。
7. Section 6 对“inventory measures trust”的主张过度。这里其实做的是一个 exploratory factor analysis，且题项来自 interaction experience inventory，不是新 trust scale 的完整验证；没有报告 reliability、measurement invariance、confirmatory validation，Table 2 里甚至把单个题项直接当成因子代表。
8. 存在多处统计或表述错误，会削弱可信度。最明显的包括：Study 2 中 `p=0.12` 却被写成 significant；5.5 把 interaction affordance 与 data complexity 的主效应解释写反；Study 1 有若干 `SE=0.97` 级别的数值疑似小数点错误；4.11 把 interaction experience 误写成 trust ratings；Study 4 / Study 5 的编号残留会让读者怀疑文稿整合还未完成。

## English Review

### A. High-Priority Logical Conflicts and Takeaway Problems

1. The manuscript is inconsistent about the number of studies. The abstract says `two crowdsourced studies`, the introduction says `three studies`, Section 3 again says `two online, crowdsourced studies`, and Section 6 says `across all three studies`. A reader cannot tell what the third study is or where the factor-analysis data came from.
2. The paper repeatedly overstates the claim that interaction helps trust regardless of quality or affordance. The abstract, Section 3, Section 7, and Section 8 all imply that any interaction helps, even buggy interaction. That is not what the results show. Study 1 reports `Full Interaction > Buggy / No Interaction`, and `Buggy` is not significantly higher than `No Interaction`. Study 2 also shows `click / hover > animation`.
3. Claims about decision making, calibrated decisions, and trustworthy interpretation are too strong. The studies mostly show changes in subjective trust, confidence, and interaction experience, while the prediction tasks are mostly null or near-null.
4. The claim that `show-one` increases `confidence` is not adequately supported in the paper. The abstract, discussion, and design implications say that revealing less information at a time improves trust and confidence, but Study 2 does not present the corresponding confidence analysis.
5. Several sentences state mechanisms rather than associations. Phrases such as `through reducing perceptual load`, `user agency calibrates trust`, and `interaction improves trust by giving users control` all sound causal, but the paper does not directly measure perceptual load and does not run mediation or mechanism analyses.
6. The fixed-first baseline trial creates an order confound. In both studies, the baseline comes first, so baseline is mixed with practice, familiarity, and sequence effects. Adding a baseline indicator later does not fully solve that problem.
7. Section 6 overclaims what the inventory can do. The paper runs an exploratory factor analysis on an adapted interaction-experience instrument; it does not validate a new trust scale. Reliability, invariance, and confirmatory validation are missing, and Table 2 reduces each new factor to a single item.
8. There are several reporting errors that weaken credibility: `p=0.12` is described as significant, the main-effect explanations in 5.5 are swapped, some Study 1 `SE=0.97` values appear to be decimal-point errors, 4.11 accidentally says `trust ratings` instead of `interaction experience ratings`, and the `Study 4 / Study 5` numbering leftovers make the draft look insufficiently integrated.

### C. Recommended Revision Order

1. First make the study count, study numbering, and data provenance fully consistent.
2. Then retract all overstrong takeaways: remove `regardless of interaction quality`, remove unsupported `confidence` claims in Study 2, and replace mechanism language with association language unless the mechanism is actually tested.
3. Fix all reporting errors: `p=0.12`, the swapped main-effect interpretations in 5.5, mixed-up labels (`trust` vs `interaction experience`), and suspicious `SE=0.97` values.
4. Reframe the inventory section explicitly as a `preliminary exploratory instrument adaptation`, and strengthen the limitations accordingly.
5. Finally, clean up grammar, terminology, and ambiguous constructs, especially `agency`, `trust`, `confidence`, and `visual complexity`.
